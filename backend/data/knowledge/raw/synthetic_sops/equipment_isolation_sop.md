# Synthetic Equipment Isolation Procedure

> SYNTHETIC PROTOTYPE SOP — NOT FOR REAL INDUSTRIAL USE

## Purpose

Provide a prototype sequence for isolating a pump, valve, or connected system before maintenance.

## Preconditions

- The work party and operations representative agree on the equipment boundary and isolation plan.
- Energy sources, stored pressure, electrical supply, process connections, and remote starts are identified.
- The approved isolation, lockout, and tagout process is available at the worksite.

## Required checks

Apply and document each required isolation. Verify the expected zero-energy condition using the approved method. Confirm drains, vents, interlocks, and adjacent equipment do not create an unrecognized path.

## Suspension or stop-work conditions

Stop work when an isolation is incomplete, a valve position is uncertain, pressure returns, equipment identification is unclear, or the work scope changes. Treat an unverified isolation as unsafe until the responsible operations role resolves it.

## Human approvals and restart

Only authorized operations and maintenance roles may approve handback and restart after the work area is clear and the permit is closed or reauthorized. No software recommendation directly operates equipment.

## Limitations

This synthetic checklist is not a replacement for the facility isolation standard or engineering approval.
